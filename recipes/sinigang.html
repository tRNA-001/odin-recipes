Sinigang

Of the many soups and stews that proliferate Filipino cuisine, it can be sure that sinigang has no rival. Many meals, both in childhood and adulthood, have been spent sitting in front of the familiarly flavorful broth. Its inviting aroma belies a flavor unlike no other — tart, savory, and refreshing, one can’t help but fall in love with it! With many heralding it as the national Filipino food, it comes as no surprise that its reputation has spread far and wide across the world.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • unripe tamarind
  • water spinach
  • string beans
  • tomatoes (chopped)
  • okra
  • 2 pcs eggplant (sliced)
  • 1 medium daikon radish
  • long green chili
  • fish sauce and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Extract the flavors from tamarind by boiling them in 2 quarts of water for 40 minutes to 1 hour. Remove the solid parts of tamarind by using a strainer. Squeeze the tamarind into the strainer to extract more flavor.

  2. Pour the tamarind broth into a pot and boil. Then add the pork belly and cover to boil. Skim-off the floating parts of the mixture. Add fish sauce to taste. Cover and simmer until the pork belly becomes tender. This can take between 45 to 90 minutes depending on the quality of the meat.

  3. Add the daikon radish and eggplants. Simmer in low heat for 5 minutes. Then add long green chili, string beans, and all other remaining vegetables except the water spinach. Simmer for another 3 minutes.

  4. Add the stalks of water spinach and season the dish with fish sauce and black pepper. After 2 minutes, add the water spinach leaves. Cover and turn the heat off. The leaves will be cooked with the residual heat in the pot. Serve after 3 to 5 minutes and enjoy!
